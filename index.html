Unju Lee

Unju Lee is a South Korean sculptor based in NY.

He received his M.F.A. at Pratt Institute and officially began his métier in sculpture in 2001.
Since then he has done a variety of exhibitions ranging from O.K. Harris, Broadway Windows,
Washington Square Windows, New York Korean Cultural Center,
and Mount Beacon Fine Arts.

The History

My first collections began with cardboard
and my works were confined by the distinctive square shape I found so charming.
In that way I created the 365 Days Collection, and in that way
I created other stand alone pieces.

Then I began to experiment with paper and assorted metals,
which kept the natural, organic feeling of the cardboard, but also gave it
the synthetic quality characteristic of the new era.

Lately I've been working on redefining these borders,
and constraining my pieces using different shapes.
Be on the lookout for updates on a new collection!
